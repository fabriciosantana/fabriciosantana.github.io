# NextPM Opportunity: Dynamics 365 Sales CRM Migration Project Manager

## Original opportunity

NextPM is seeking a highly skilled Freelance Project Manager to lead an international CRM migration project. The role is crucial for the end-to-end management of a CRM migration, specifically focusing on Microsoft Dynamics 365 Sales. The Project Manager will serve as the primary liaison between business stakeholders, technical teams, and external partners. This is a hands-on position with a strong emphasis on delivery in a fast-paced, results-oriented environment.

Must-have requirements:

- Available to attend 2-3 days in Porto's office, Portugal.
- Professional or native level Portuguese.
- Freelance collaboration, B2B.
- Strong experience as a data project manager.

Key responsibilities include:

- Leading all project phases of the CRM migration.
- Coordinating local and international stakeholders.
- Defining and managing project scope, timelines, risks, budget, and dependencies.
- Ensuring alignment between business requirements and technical solutions.
- Overseeing functional analysis, system configuration, data migration, testing, go-live, and hypercare support.
- Facilitating governance through status meetings, steering committees, and workshops.
- Reporting on KPIs, risks, issues, and progress.
- Ensuring delivery according to timeline, quality, objectives, and budget.

Requirements include at least 3 years as a Project Manager in data-intensive environments, CRM implementation or migration experience, Portuguese and English proficiency, strong stakeholder management, international/cross-functional experience, autonomy, and results orientation.

Desirable experience includes Microsoft Dynamics 365 Sales, hybrid Agile + Waterfall delivery, and the Microsoft ecosystem.

## Opportunity assessment

The main challenge is not only coordinating a CRM migration timeline. The important part is ensuring that business requirements, Dynamics 365 Sales configuration, data migration, testing, go-live, and hypercare move together under one controlled governance model.

CRM migrations usually fail or become painful when the migration is treated as a tool replacement instead of an operational change. The visible request is for a Project Manager, but the hidden delivery problem includes:

- source CRM assessment and data readiness;
- data mapping, cleansing, deduplication, and ownership decisions;
- business process alignment with Dynamics 365 Sales configuration;
- integration dependencies with reporting, marketing, finance, ERP, identity, or other systems;
- stakeholder decision latency across local and international teams;
- testing strategy for configuration, data migration, integrations, permissions, reports, and user workflows;
- go-live planning, rollback criteria, cutover responsibilities, and hypercare support;
- adoption risks when sales teams need to change daily workflows;
- budget and timeline pressure in a fast-paced environment.

The opportunity has strong seriousness signals: NextPM mentions PMO practices, steering committees, workshops, KPIs, risks, issues, dependencies, data-intensive project management, Dynamics 365 Sales, B2B collaboration, and international delivery.

The main commercial risk is the onsite requirement. Availability for 2-3 days per week in Porto is marked as a must-have. If that cannot be met, the opportunity should not be pursued unless NextPM is open to a mostly remote model with planned onsite workshops.

## Recommended commercial framing

The safest commercial path is to propose a short Discovery Sprint / transition assessment before committing to a long engagement. This fits the repository methodology while adapting it to a project-management role rather than a fixed software build.

Suggested immediate step:

- 1-week Discovery Sprint / project transition assessment.
- Review current project phase, stakeholders, scope, migration plan, risks, timelines, data readiness, governance model, and onsite expectations.
- Produce a practical CRM migration delivery plan covering governance cadence, critical path, risk register, dependency map, reporting model, decision framework, testing/go-live/hypercare approach, and recommended engagement model.

Possible engagement after discovery:

- B2B project leadership engagement, part-time or full-time depending on expected onsite days, project phase, and team needs.
- Fixed 4-week delivery-management cycle if NextPM wants a bounded initial engagement to stabilize governance, migration planning, reporting, and go-live readiness.
- Longer monthly engagement if the project requires end-to-end leadership through design, configuration, data migration, testing, cutover, and hypercare.

Suggested package logic:

- Discovery Sprint: USD 500 for 1 week, or equivalent EUR amount if NextPM prefers local invoicing.
- Product Acceleration-style delivery leadership: USD 4,000 for a 4-week bounded cycle focused on governance, migration planning, stakeholder alignment, risk control, and go-live readiness.
- Scale & Intelligence-style delivery leadership: USD 6,000 for 4 weeks if the migration is already in a critical phase with complex data, multiple integrations, high stakeholder pressure, strict reporting, and significant cutover risk.

Because this is a B2B freelance PM role, the final commercial model may need to be converted into a daily or monthly rate after discovery.

## Criteria that may increase scope or require stronger engagement

- Multiple countries, business units, or sales processes.
- Poor source data quality, duplicate customer/account/contact records, or unclear ownership rules.
- Complex integrations with ERP, marketing automation, reporting, identity, or external tools.
- Tight go-live deadline or regulatory/business deadline.
- Lack of business ownership for data cleansing and acceptance testing.
- No existing test strategy for migration validation, user acceptance, and cutover.
- High dependency on external partners without clear RACI.
- Need to rebuild governance, reporting, and decision cadence from scratch.
- High onsite expectation without travel logistics clarified.
