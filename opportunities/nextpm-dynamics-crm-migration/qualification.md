# Qualification

## Decision

`pursue discovery only`

## Summary

This is a strong professional fit because the opportunity requires senior project leadership for a data-intensive international CRM migration, stakeholder coordination, scope/risk/budget control, governance, testing, go-live, and hypercare. It also aligns with Fabricio Santana's background in technical leadership, complex systems, data consistency, delivery governance, and cross-functional coordination.

The opportunity should only be pursued if the onsite requirement can be met or negotiated: NextPM lists availability for 2-3 days per week in Porto, Portugal, as a must-have. If that requirement is non-negotiable and Fabricio cannot attend onsite, the recommendation becomes `skip`.

## Qualification assessment

- Budget signal or likely ability to pay: moderate to strong. The client is a consulting/project organization hiring B2B for an international CRM migration, which suggests a meaningful professional services budget.
- Clarity of scope: moderate. The role is clear at a management level, but the actual project status, migration volume, timeline, team structure, data quality, and decision authority are unknown.
- Fit with positioning: strong if framed as senior delivery leadership for a data-intensive CRM migration. The fit is less about hands-on coding and more about technical project management, delivery governance, migration risk control, and stakeholder alignment.
- Urgency and business pain: likely high. CRM migrations affect sales operations, reporting, data quality, adoption, integrations, and business continuity.
- Access to decision maker: unknown. The role requires liaison with business stakeholders, technical teams, and external partners, but the hiring process may be through NextPM rather than the end client.
- Risk of uncontrolled scope: high. CRM migrations often expand through data cleansing, field mapping, duplicate handling, integration changes, reporting gaps, training, testing, cutover dependencies, and post-go-live support.
- Seriousness of client signals: strong. The posting is specific about Dynamics 365 Sales, PMO practices, governance, international coordination, B2B collaboration, and expected deliverables.
- Likelihood of competing only on low price: low to medium. The onsite requirement, language requirements, CRM migration experience, and international stakeholder management reduce pure low-price competition.

## Recommended commercial framing

Recommend responding with a senior delivery-management proposal, not a generic project-manager pitch. The strongest angle is that a CRM migration succeeds when scope, data migration, functional design, system configuration, testing, cutover, adoption, and hypercare are managed as one controlled delivery system.

The immediate commercial step should be a short Discovery Sprint / transition assessment to confirm:

- current project phase and migration deadline;
- Dynamics 365 Sales scope and module boundaries;
- source CRM and data migration complexity;
- stakeholder map and governance cadence;
- team structure across business, technical teams, and external partners;
- migration risks, open issues, dependencies, and critical path;
- onsite expectations in Porto and remote collaboration model;
- engagement model, availability, daily rate or fixed discovery budget, and decision process.

Because this is a staff-augmentation / project leadership role, a monthly B2B engagement or day-rate model may fit better than the standard fixed 4-week delivery packages. The repository methodology still applies by using Discovery first and converting uncertainty into a concrete delivery plan, governance model, and engagement proposal.

## Main sales angle

The proposal should show that the main challenge is not only managing meetings and timelines. The real work is protecting the migration outcome: keeping business requirements, Dynamics 365 configuration, data migration, testing, go-live, hypercare, stakeholder decisions, risks, and budget under one disciplined governance model.

The application should emphasize:

- Portuguese and English communication;
- international stakeholder coordination;
- technical credibility with data-intensive systems;
- structured reporting, risks, issues, KPIs, and steering committees;
- practical go-live and hypercare control;
- autonomy and results orientation;
- conditional clarity about Porto onsite availability.
