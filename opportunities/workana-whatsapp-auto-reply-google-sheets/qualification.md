# Qualification

## Decision

`pursue discovery only`

## Summary

This is a strong fit with Fabricio Santana's positioning because it combines backend development, API integration, automation, workflow routing, operational reliability, and technical recommendations. The safest commercial move is to sell the Discovery Sprint first while making the implementation path explicit, because the client wants both implementation and guidance on the best WhatsApp-compliant architecture, while the exact provider, message volume, routing rules, data model, and support workflow are still unknown.

## Qualification assessment

- Budget signal or likely ability to pay: moderate to strong. The posting signals more than R$ 200 per hour, which suggests room for a serious implementation if scope is clearly framed.
- Clarity of scope: moderate. The core capabilities are clear, but the WhatsApp provider, escalation rules, content model, expected message volume, and operational workflow are not.
- Fit with positioning: strong. This is a productized automation and integration opportunity with compliance and operational considerations.
- Urgency and business pain: likely real. The request is tied directly to customer service efficiency and communication throughput.
- Access to decision maker: unknown.
- Risk of uncontrolled scope: medium to high. WhatsApp projects often expand into CRM integration, human handoff consoles, analytics, campaign messaging, AI layers, templates, and audit requirements.
- Seriousness of client signals: good. The request is specific, operational, and technically grounded.
- Likelihood of competing only on low price: medium. There will likely be low-cost unofficial WhatsApp automation offers, so the proposal should differentiate through compliance, reliability, and maintainability.

## Recommended commercial framing

Recommend selling the Discovery Sprint as the immediate next step, with a clear path to implementation immediately afterward. Use discovery to confirm whether the correct architecture is:

- official WhatsApp Business Platform or approved BSP integration;
- rule-based FAQ responder or a broader chatbot workflow;
- Google Sheets as the primary operational datastore or only a support layer for logging and dynamic content;
- Essential Delivery or Product Acceleration for the first implementation cycle.

The likely package after discovery is:

- `Essential Delivery` if the scope is a bounded FAQ bot with basic routing and Google Sheets integration.
- `Product Acceleration` if the scope includes richer escalation rules, stronger observability, templating, admin workflows, or multi-agent operational routing.

## Main sales angle

The proposal should make clear that the main challenge is not only replying to WhatsApp messages. The real work is building a compliant and reliable customer-service workflow where incoming messages, routing rules, fallback handling, Sheets integration, logging, and human escalation all work together without relying on brittle unofficial automation. The business value should be explicit: faster response times, more consistent answers, lower manual support load, better handoff to agents, and better visibility into customer interactions.
