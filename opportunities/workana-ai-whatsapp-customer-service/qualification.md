# Qualification

## Decision

`pursue discovery only`

## Summary

This is a strong fit with Fabricio Santana's positioning because it combines AI application design, backend integration, NLP, workflow automation, WhatsApp Business API integration, human handoff, and operational reliability. The safest commercial move is to sell the Discovery Sprint first because the request asks for an AI-powered system, but the real implementation boundary depends on the provider setup, knowledge sources, escalation rules, response-risk tolerance, expected message volume, and support workflow.

## Qualification assessment

- Budget signal or likely ability to pay: moderate to strong. The request combines AI, NLP, integration, deployment, and customer-service automation, which suggests a more serious scope than a simple chatbot setup.
- Clarity of scope: moderate. The objective is clear, but the WhatsApp provider, knowledge base, training data quality, model approach, escalation criteria, compliance constraints, and operational workflow are not.
- Fit with positioning: strong. This is a good fit for APIs, automation, AI applications, backend workflows, testing, deployment, and maintainable delivery.
- Urgency and business pain: likely real. The project is tied directly to response time, support efficiency, and customer-service consistency.
- Access to decision maker: unknown.
- Risk of uncontrolled scope: high. AI support projects often expand into CRM integration, full agent consoles, analytics, multilingual support, fine-tuning, retrieval pipelines, dashboards, quality review workflows, and broad customer-service redesign.
- Seriousness of client signals: good. The request is specific about intent recognition, automated responses, WhatsApp Business API, training data, and escalation to human agents.
- Likelihood of competing only on low price: medium. There will likely be low-cost offers using unofficial WhatsApp automation or overpromised LLM wrappers without operational controls. The proposal should differentiate through compliance, bounded scope, observability, and maintainability.

## Recommended commercial framing

Recommend selling the Discovery Sprint as the immediate next step, with a clear path to implementation right after it. Use discovery to confirm whether the correct first version is:

- rule-based plus AI-assisted intent classification;
- retrieval-based FAQ answering using approved business content;
- a broader generative assistant with strict guardrails and human escalation;
- one Product Acceleration cycle or one Scale \& Intelligence cycle.

The likely package after discovery is:

- `Product Acceleration` if the first version is a bounded AI-assisted support workflow with official WhatsApp Business API integration, intent recognition, approved response sources, logging, and handoff.
- `Scale & Intelligence` if the scope includes broader NLP behavior, multilingual flows, richer knowledge retrieval, stricter observability and evaluation, admin tooling, or more demanding reliability requirements.

## Main sales angle

The proposal should make clear that the main challenge is not only connecting an AI model to WhatsApp. The real work is building a reliable support workflow where message intake, intent recognition, approved answer generation, confidence thresholds, escalation to human agents, conversation logging, and provider compliance work together without creating operational or reputational risk.

The business value should be explicit:

- faster response times;
- more consistent answers;
- lower repetitive support load;
- controlled human escalation for uncertain cases;
- clearer visibility into support interactions and automation quality.
